INTERNATIONAL SUMMER CONFERENCE: LIVING WITH SAI

JULY 17-21, 2019, PRASANTHI NILAYAM

It is generally not possible for anything to change or transform by itself, without the help of external associations. Even a newly born learns to walk, talk, run, read and write only with the help of its mother and the love and affection the latter shows...

Thus, the kind of sacred life, which you wish to lead and the aspirations which you have in your mind about the type of life, which you want to live, can be achieved, if you are in the company of good people…

Therefore, you should make an attempt to join the Satsang and derive all the benefits that accrue from joining good company. Good company leads you to detachment and by detachment you will achieve self-realisation.

Excerpts from Divine Discourse, Summer Course in Spirituality and Indian Culture,
May 24, 1973

The SSIO is delighted to introduce SAI (Self-Awareness and Inspiration) Summer Series: An (annual) International Summer Conference in Prasanthi Nilayam, the Abode of Supreme Peace. The first conference themed “Living with Sai” will take place from 17th to 21st July 2019. We extend a loving invitation to all interested participants to register for this unique event.

These international summer conferences are a string of events focused on personal transformation topics and activities. Typical programmes include: Group spiritual practices, eminent speakers, unique experience-sharing sessions, workshops, service activities and guided visits to key sites around Prasanthi Nilayam, among others. A parallel programme for children of participants will also be provided. Registration is required.

I have a "Task": To foster all mankind and ensure for all of them lives full of ananda (bliss). I have a "Vow": To lead all who stray away from the straight path again into goodness and save them. I am attached to a "work" that I love: To remove the sufferings of the poor and grant them what they lack. I have a "reason to be proud", for I rescue all who worship and adore me, aright. I have my definition of the "devotion" I expect: Those devoted to me have to treat joy and grief, gain and loss, with equal fortitude. This means that I will never give up those who attach themselves to me.

- Sri Sathya Sai Baba
